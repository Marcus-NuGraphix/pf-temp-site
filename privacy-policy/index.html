Privacy Policy

This policy explains how The Portuguese Forum collects, processes, stores, and protects personal information across our website and mobile app.

    Privacy Policy

    Effective Date: February 15, 2024

    1. Introduction

    Welcome to Portuguese Forum (“Portuguese Forum”, “we”, “us”, or “our”). This Privacy Policy governs your use of our website (https://portugueseforum.org.za) and our mobile application (“Portuguese Forum App”) available on Android and Apple platforms (collectively, the “Service”). This policy explains how we collect, safeguard, and use your personal data—including sensitive data—and your rights regarding that data. By accessing or using our Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy and our Terms and Conditions. If you do not agree, please do not use our Service.

    2. Definitions

    For the purposes of this Privacy Policy:

    • Service: The Portuguese Forum website and mobile application.
    • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
    • Sensitive Data: Categories of Personal Data that require additional protection, such as passport details, employment information, government-issued identifiers, and other sensitive information.
    • Usage Data: Information automatically collected from your use of our Service (e.g., IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages visited, and diagnostic data).
    • Cookies: Small data files stored on your device that help us enhance and personalize your experience.
    • Data Controller: Portuguese Forum, which determines the purposes and means of processing your personal data.
    • Data Processor/Service Provider: Third parties that process data on our behalf.
    • Data Subject: Any individual whose personal data is collected and processed.

    3. Information Collection and Use

    We collect a variety of information to provide and improve our Service. This includes Personal Data, Sensitive Data, Usage Data, and data collected through Cookies and other tracking technologies from both our website and mobile app.

    4. Types of Data Collected

    4.1. Personal Data

    While using our Service, we may ask you to provide certain personally identifiable information, including but not limited to:

    • Email address
    • First name and last name
    • Phone number
    • Postal address (including country, state/province, ZIP/Postal code, and city)
    • Cookies and Usage Data

    4.2. Sensitive Data

    Where relevant to the functionality of our Service, we may collect Sensitive Data, including but not limited to:

    • Demographic details (e.g., age, sex, date of birth)
    • Identification details (e.g., passport information, citizenship)
    • Employment and education details (e.g., professional training, employment agreements, compensation details)
    • Other sensitive information necessary for the operation or personalization of our Service

    4.3. Usage Data

    We automatically collect information that your browser or mobile app sends whenever you access our Service. This may include:

    • IP address, browser type, and browser version
    • Device identifiers, operating system details, and unique device IDs
    • Pages visited, time spent on pages, and other diagnostic data

    4.4. Location Data

    If you grant permission, we may collect and store information about your geographic location to enable location-based features and improve our Service. You can control this through your device settings.

    4.5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

    We use cookies and similar technologies to track activity on our Service and enhance your experience. Examples include:

    • Session Cookies: To operate our Service
    • Preference Cookies: To remember your settings and preferences
    • Security Cookies: For security purposes
    • Advertising Cookies: To serve relevant advertisements based on your interests

    You can manage your cookie preferences via your browser or device settings. Please note that disabling cookies may affect certain functionalities of our Service.

    5. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR Compliance)

    If you are located in the European Union (EU) or European Economic Area (EEA), our processing of your personal data is based on one or more of the following legal bases:

    • Consent: Where you have provided explicit consent for the collection and use of your personal data.
    • Contractual Necessity: To fulfill our contractual obligations with you.
    • Legal Obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations.
    • Legitimate Interests: Where processing is necessary for our legitimate interests, provided these do not override your rights and freedoms.

    6. Use of Data

    Portuguese Forum uses the collected data for various purposes, including:

    1. Providing and maintaining our Service.
    2. Notifying you about changes to our Service.
    3. Allowing you to participate in interactive features when you choose to do so.
    4. Providing customer support.
    5. Analyzing usage to improve our Service.
    6. Monitoring and ensuring the security and functionality of our Service.
    7. Fulfilling contractual obligations and enforcing our rights.
    8. Sending account-related notifications (e.g., subscription updates, renewal notices).
    9. Providing news, special offers, and information about goods, services, or events (with your consent, where applicable).
    10. Any other purpose disclosed to you when you provide the information, or with your consent.

    7. Retention of Data

    We retain your Personal Data and Sensitive Data only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy. This period may be extended to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our policies. Usage Data is generally retained for a shorter period unless needed for security or analytical purposes.

    8. Data Transfers

    Your personal data, including Sensitive Data, may be transferred to and maintained on servers located outside your jurisdiction (for example, in South Africa), where data protection laws may differ. By using our Service, you consent to such transfers. We take all reasonable steps—including the use of standard contractual clauses or other safeguards—to ensure your data is treated securely and in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

    9. Disclosure of Data

    We may disclose your personal data in the following circumstances:

    1. For Law Enforcement: If required by law or in response to a valid request by public authorities.
    2. Business Transactions: In connection with mergers, acquisitions, or asset sales, wherein your data may be transferred.
    3. Other Disclosures: To:
      • Our subsidiaries and affiliates.
      • Contractors, service providers, or other third parties who assist us in operating our Service.
      • Fulfill the purpose for which you provided the information.
      • Include your company’s logo on our website (if applicable).
      • With your consent or as otherwise disclosed at the time of collection.
      • Protect the rights, property, or safety of Portuguese Forum, our users, or others.

    10. Security of Data

    We strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal data; however, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

    11. Your Rights Under GDPR

    If you are an EU/EEA resident, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

    1. Access: The right to access the personal data we hold about you.
    2. Rectification: The right to correct or update inaccurate or incomplete data.
    3. Objection: The right to object to or restrict the processing of your personal data.
    4. Data Portability: The right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
    5. Withdrawal of Consent: The right to withdraw your consent at any time (where processing is based on consent).

    To exercise these rights, please contact us at info@portugueseforum.org.za. We may require verification of your identity before processing your request.

    12. Your Rights Under CalOPPA

    For California residents, we agree to the following:

    1. You may visit our site anonymously.
    2. Our Privacy Policy is clearly accessible and includes the term “Privacy.”
    3. You will be notified of any privacy policy changes on this page.
    4. You may request changes to your personal information by contacting us at info@portugueseforum.org.za.

    13. Your Rights Under CCPA

    If you are a California resident, you have the following rights:

    1. Right to Know: The right to request information about the personal data we have collected about you, including:
      • Categories of data collected.
      • Sources from which your data is collected.
      • The business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling your data.
      • Categories of third parties with whom your data is shared.
      • Specific pieces of personal data we have collected.
      • A list of categories of data sold or disclosed for a business purpose (if applicable).
    2. Right to Delete: The right to request deletion of your personal data.
    3. Right to Opt-Out: The right to request that we stop selling or sharing your personal data (Note: We do not sell your personal data).

    Requests can be made up to two times within a rolling 12-month period. To exercise these rights, please contact us at info@portugueseforum.org.za.

    14. Service Providers

    We may engage third-party companies and individuals (“Service Providers”) to facilitate our Service, perform related services, or assist in analyzing our Service usage. These Service Providers have access to your personal data only to perform tasks on our behalf and are obligated not to disclose or use it for any other purpose.

    15. Analytics

    We may use third-party Service Providers to monitor and analyze the use of our Service to improve its functionality and security.

    16. CI/CD Tools

    We may employ third-party tools to automate our development and deployment processes. These tools may collect usage data to help us improve the Service.

    17. Behavioral Remarketing

    We may use remarketing services to display advertisements on third-party websites after you have visited our Service. This process may involve cookies and similar tracking technologies to serve ads that are relevant to your interests based on your previous interactions with our Service.

    18. Links to Other Sites

    Our Service may contain links to external websites not operated by us. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of those websites, as we have no control over their content or practices.

    19. Children’s Privacy

    Our Service is not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 18. If we learn that we have inadvertently collected such data, we will take steps to delete it as soon as possible. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately.

    20. Changes to This Privacy Policy

    We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When changes are made, we will update the “Effective Date” at the top of this document and, where appropriate, notify you via email or a prominent notice on our Service. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

    21. Contact Us

    If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us at:

    Email: info@portugueseforum.org.za

    Should you wish to have your data removed from our database please fill out the form here.

    If applicable, we may also provide additional contact details for our Data Protection Officer.
